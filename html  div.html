Arshdeep was found guilty of breaching Article 2.9 of the ICC Code of Conduct for Players and Player Support Personnel, which relates to “throwing a ball (or any other item of cricket equipment) at or near a player in an inappropriate and/or dangerous manner during an International Match.”Arshdeep Singh (born February 5, 1999) has rapidly emerged as one of India’s premier left-arm medium-fast bowlers, specializing in white-ball cricket with a reputation for incredible composure under pressure. Hailing from Guna, Madhya Pradesh, and representing Punjab in domestic cricket, Arshdeep’s journey to the international stage is defined by his consistency, lethal death-overs bowling, and a penchant for delivering crucial wickets. He first gained recognition as a key member of India's victorious 2018 Under-19 World Cup squad. His Indian Premier League (IPL) journey began with the Punjab Kings in 2019, where he was bought for his base price of ₹20 lakh, eventually becoming an indispensable asset and leading wicket-taker for the franchise. Arshdeep made his international T20I debut for India in July 2022 against England, starting his career with a rare maiden over. Since his debut, he has established himself as a "death-overs specialist," renowned for his pinpoint yorkers, ability to generate swing, and calm temperament. His performances in high-pressure situations have been stellar, notably in the 2024 T20 World Cup, where he played a pivotal role in India’s title-winning campaign, finishing as the joint-highest wicket-taker with 17 wickets. Furthermore, he made history in September 2025 by becoming the first Indian bowler to reach 100 T20I wickets, doing so in just 64 games. He was named the ICC Men's T20I Cricketer of the Year for 2024, cementing his place as a superstar in the shortest format. His ability to operate in both the powerplay and death overs has made him a vital part of India's white-ball setups, and he has also demonstrated his skill in ODIs, taking a five-wicket haul against South Africa in 2023. Beyond his technical skills, Arshdeep is known for his dedication, frequently practicing accuracy over raw speed. He has also shown potential in the longer format, having played County Cricket for Kent. As of 2025–26, he continues to be a key figure for the Indian national team and was retained by Punjab Kings for a substantial amount, highlighting his value as a premier fast bowler in the T20 format.
